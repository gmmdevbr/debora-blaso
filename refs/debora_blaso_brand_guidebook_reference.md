# Débora Blaso – Brand Guidebook (O Cérebro)

> **Purpose**: Reference document for engineering, design systems, UI, UX, branding, and content decisions.
> Optimised for use by coding agents (React, Tailwind, SEO).

---

## 1. Brand Essence

### Brand Positioning
Débora Blaso is a clinical psychologist who integrates **scientific psychology** with **fashion and aesthetics**, creating a unique positioning that blends emotional intelligence, self-image, and identity.

The brand communicates:
- Authority and scientific credibility
- Emotional safety and trust
- Editorial, high-end aesthetic
- Human warmth with intellectual depth

### Core Concept
**"The brain"** as the symbolic and conceptual centre of the brand:
- Rational + emotional
- Science + sensitivity
- Structure + creativity

---

## 2. Brand Personality

### Archetypes
- **The Sage** – knowledge, science, reflection
- **The Caregiver** – empathy, safety, emotional presence

### Personality Traits
- Calm
- Sophisticated
- Grounded
- Trustworthy
- Reflective
- Elegant, never flashy

Tone should never be:
- Aggressive
- Infantilised
- Overly clinical
- Salesy

---

## 3. Visual Identity Principles

### Visual Direction
- Editorial / Magazine style
- High contrast between light and dark areas
- Organic shapes and asymmetry
- Space, silence, and breathing room

### Design Keywords
- Organic
- Cerebral
- Soft contrast
- Natural elegance
- Sensory calm

---

## 4. Colour System

Primary palette communicates nature, intellect, and emotional grounding.

### Core Colours
- **Monstera Green** – authority, nature, stability
- **Summer Sand** – calm, warmth, background neutrality
- **Walnut / Terracotta** – human warmth, accent
- **Ginkgo / Soft Beige** – highlight, softness
- **Muted Grey** – secondary text

Usage rules:
- Prefer muted, earthy tones
- Avoid saturated or neon colours
- Backgrounds should feel tactile and calm

---

## 5. Typography System

### Typographic Roles

#### Display / Headlines
- Serif-based
- Express authority and editorial presence
- Used for:
  - Hero titles
  - Section headers
  - Quotes

#### Body Text
- Condensed sans-serif
- High readability
- Calm rhythm
- Used for:
  - Paragraphs
  - Descriptions
  - Long-form content

#### Script / Accent
- Used sparingly
- Emotional and human touch
- Used for:
  - Signatures
  - Short emphasis
  - Decorative highlights

### Typographic Principles
- Clear hierarchy
- Generous line-height
- Avoid dense blocks of text

---

## 6. Imagery & Photography

### Image Style
- Natural light
- Editorial framing
- Soft shadows
- Human presence without forced poses

### Cropping & Shapes
- Organic masks
- Asymmetry preferred
- Avoid rigid rectangular framing where possible

Images should convey:
- Presence
- Calm
- Depth
- Authenticity

---

## 7. Graphic Elements

### Organic Shapes
- Inspired by nature and the brain
- Used as masks and containers

### Texture
- Subtle noise / grain
- Adds tactile, printed feel
- Should be very low opacity

---

## 8. Communication & Copy

### Language Style
- First person or close third person
- Warm but professional
- Simple language with intellectual depth

### Writing Principles
- Short paragraphs
- Clear intention
- Emotional clarity
- Avoid jargon when possible

---

## 9. Digital & Web Guidelines

### UX Principles
- Calm navigation
- Few but clear CTAs
- Predictable interactions
- Smooth transitions

### SEO & Content
- Semantic structure (H1–H3)
- Human-first copy
- Authority through clarity, not volume

### Interaction
- Subtle animations
- Organic movement
- No aggressive motion

---

## 10. Brand Consistency Rules

- Always prioritise clarity over decoration
- Aesthetic should support trust
- Science and emotion must coexist
- Design should never overpower content

---

## 11. Summary for Engineering Use

When implementing this brand in code:

- Prefer **semantic HTML**
- Use **editorial spacing**
- Respect typographic hierarchy
- Keep animations subtle
- Avoid visual noise

This document should guide:
- Component design
- Theme tokens
- Layout decisions
- Copy tone
